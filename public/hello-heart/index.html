How we calculate this ▾
Eligible population — We apply the CDC NHANES-derived hypertension prevalence rate to your workforce. Rates vary by industry: manufacturing (45%), government (42%), finance (41%), healthcare (38%). CDC estimates ~47% of U.S. adults have hypertension; the 42% figure accounts for working-age adults only.
Year 1 enrollment — Hello Heart's best-practice outreach benchmark yields ~30% of eligible members enrolled in Year 1 with active employer promotion.
Medical savings — The City of Fort Worth deployed Hello Heart to ~3,000 employees. Independent actuarial analysis using a matched-control methodology found $2,604 in avoided medical costs per enrolled member vs. the control group.
4:1 ROI — Across the Fort Worth cohort, for every $1 invested in the program, the employer realized $4 in claims savings. This accounts for subscription cost, outreach, and implementation.
Clinical benchmarks — A peer-reviewed JAMA Network Open study (Gazit et al., 2021) found an average 16 mmHg systolic reduction in Stage 2 hypertension users. The Fort Worth study found 74% of high-risk members improved their blood pressure classification within 12 months.
